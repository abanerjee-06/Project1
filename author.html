The author of the greatest fictional detective of all times-- Sherlock Holmes.

Arthur Conan Doyle was a British writer and medical doctor. He created this character in 1887 when he published A Study in Scarlet. The Sherlock Holmes tales amount to a total of four novels and 56 short stories.

Apart from the Sherlock Holmes series, he has written many other science fiction and fantasy short stories about Professor Challenger and humorous short stories about the Napoleonic soldier Brigadier Gerard and various plays, romances and historical novels.

Arthur Conan Doyle was born on 22 May 1859 at 11 Picardy Place, Edinburgh, Scotland. His parents were father Charles Altamont Doyle and Mary.

From 1876-1881, Doyle studied medicine at the University of Edinburgh Medical School. While studying he published short stories. His first published piece was The Mystery of Sasassa Valley

After graduating with a degree from University of Edinburgh in 1881, he was ship's surgeon on the SS Mayumba during a voyage to the West-African coast. He completed his Doctor of Medicine(M.D.) in 1885.

He had started independent practice in Portsmouth in 1882, but his practice was poor. This was the time when he returned to writing fiction.
